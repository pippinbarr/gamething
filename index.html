GAMETHING is answerphone messages between Pippin Barr and David Wolinsky that explores videogames from top to bottom. Each season explores a specific theme, such as simulation or horror, and discusses one game per episode. The physics of loneliness? Police officers who can't say sorry? Death by dysentery? GAMETHING has it all.

Pippin Barr

Pippin is an Associate Professor of Computation Arts at Concordia University in Montréal who makes experimental games about anything from performance art to chess to classic films. He's a regular jury-member for the Nuovo Award (the preeminent international experimental games prize) at the Independent Games Festival and judges for IndieCade. His forthcoming book, The Stuff Games Are Made Of (MIT Press), explores videogame design through the lens of its materials, from computation, to interfaces, to time.

David Wolinsky

David is an author, recovering critic, and creator/curator of the interview series Don't Die, preserved by Stanford. He's also a former Onion and NBC editor. If you see him, say "hi."
