Description

What are videogames? And why are they?

In every episode, experimental game designer Pippin Barr and oral historian David Wolinsky select a game to play separately. Then, they exchange answerphone messages about it until the tape runs out. The result is conversations about anything and everything (mostly everything) they find interesting about videogames.

GAMETHING explores different themes each season as a jumping off point for choosing the games, finding "small moments done well" in play, and poking at connections between life and games. Pippin puzzles through Baudrilliard's "Desert of the Real" relationship to simulator games, David works through the emotional labour in a horror role-playing game, and we both try (and sometimes actually manage) to talk about how games feel as a result of their underlying design. These are GAMETHINGS.

For those already deeply into games, every episode invites listeners to come along on an introspective playthrough of a game with fresh eyes, with special attention to that season's Big Idea. For those looking for ways into understanding games, GAMETHING is a way to indulge some curiosity about this weird and thrilling medium, with the comfort of being anchored by that same Big Idea's relevance to "real life."

History

Pippin and David have been collaborating on GAMETHING since 2018 with alphabetical audio experiments about videogames, beginning with Alone in the Dark and making it as far as Little Computer People. Seasons 1 and 2 followed this approach into poetry, musical performance, sonic collage, and more.

In late 2022, we got back into the saddle with a total reset. Instead of experimental audio, we decided to try something novel: What if we actually talked about games instead?

Features

  • Games of all kinds from throughout the history of the medium
  • Open-hearted, patient play and discussion that demystifies games and then complicates them all over again
  • The cadence of phone messages sent over time brings you along in real time as we explore a game and dig into questions it evokes

Who

GAMETHING is hosted by David Wolinsky and Pippin Barr.

David Wolinsky is an author, recovering critic, and creator/curator of Don't Die (2014-present), a Stanford-preserved and award-winning oral history series exploring alternate histories of the internet, videogames, and the re-negotiation of power made possible and inevitable by our new systems for socializing, working, and interacting. He's also a former Onion and NBC editor. If you see him, say "hi."

David is the 1995 Blockbuster store champion at Donkey Kong Country and author of the forthcoming oral history All The Rage: Conversations Processing Gamergate.

Pippin Barr an Associate Professor of Computation Arts at Concordia University in Montréal who makes experimental games about anything from performance art to chess to classic films. He's a regular jury-member for the Nuovo Award (the preeminent international experimental games prize) at the Independent Games Festival and judges for IndieCade.

Pippin's book, The Stuff Games Are Made Of (MIT Press, 2023), explores videogame design through the lens of its materials, from computation, to interfaces, to time.
