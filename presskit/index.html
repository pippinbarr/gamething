Description

Each episode, experimental game designer Pippin Barr and oral historian David Wolinsky choose a game then both play it separately and exchange answerphone messages about it until the tape runs out. The result is conversations about anything and everything (mostly everything) they find interesting about videogames.

Every season of GAMETHING tackles a theme. Season 3 is about simulators, Season 4 (already underway) is about horror. Pippin and David use the theme as a jumping off point for choosing the games, finding "small moments done well" in play, and poking at connections between life and games. You'll hear everything from Pippin trying to puzzle through how Baudrilliard's "Desert of the Real" relates to simulator games, David unpacking the exhilaration of coasting downhill in a truck with no breaks, to both trying (and sometimes actually managing) to talk about how games feel as a result of their underlying design.

For those already deeply into games, every episode invites listeners to come along on an introspective playthrough of a game with fresh eyes, with special attention to that season's Big Idea. For those looking for ways into understanding games, GAMETHING is a way to indulge some curiosity about this weird and thrilling medium, with the comfort of being anchored by that same Big Idea's relevance to "real life." What are videogames? And why are they?

History

Pippin and David have been collaborating on GAMETHING since 2018 with alphabetical audio experiments about videogames, beginning with Alone in the Dark and making it as far as Little Computer People. Seasons 1 and 2 followed this approach into poetry, musical performance, sonic collage, and more.

In late 2022, we got back into the saddle with a total reset. Instead of experimental audio, we decided to try something novel: What if we actually talked about games instead?

Who

GAMETHING is hosted by David Wolinsky and Pippin Barr.

David Wolinsky is an author, recovering critic, and creator/curator of Don't Die (2014-present), a Stanford-preserved and award-winning oral history series exploring alternate histories of the internet, videogames, and the re-negotiation of power made possible and inevitable by our new systems for socializing, working, and interacting. He's also a former Onion and NBC editor. If you see him, say "hi."

David is the 1995 Blockbuster store champion at Donkey Kong Country and author of the forthcoming oral history All The Rage: Conversations Processing Gamergate.
